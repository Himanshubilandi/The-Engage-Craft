Before You Write A Single Word
The Mindset Shift
// Core Truth

You are not begging for work. You are a growth partner showing up with a specific idea for their specific problem. The difference is everything. Brands ignore "we offer marketing services." Brands reply to "I noticed your 600K followers are getting 0.5% engagement — here's why and here's the fix."

🎯
Lead with THEIR problem
Never open with who you are. Open with what you noticed about them. They only care about themselves.
💡
Give the idea for free
Drop 1 specific viral campaign idea in the first message. If it's good, they'll want to know who you are.
🔬
Show you did homework
Mention their follower count, a specific post, or a product they just launched. Generic pitches = instant ignore.
📩
One ask only
Don't ask for a call AND a proposal AND a meeting. Ask for ONE thing: "Would this be worth exploring?"
01
How To Frame Aarush Bhola As Your Proof
No Portfolio? No Problem.
// The Reframe

You ran content strategy for a creator's fan pages. That is real work, real results. You don't need to oversell it — just frame it correctly. Here's how to position it depending on what you can honestly say about results.

// What to say in pitches
The exact line to use
"We recently ran a content campaign for creator Aarush Bhola's fan pages — focused on driving engagement and reach through meme-native formats. Happy to share what worked if you'd like."

That's it. Simple, honest, creates curiosity. They'll ask — and that's the opening you want.
// If you have numbers
Use them directly
If posts got X likes, X shares, X reach — say that. "Campaigns averaged 15K reach per post." Even small numbers are better than none.
// If you don't have numbers
Sell the process
"We developed the content calendar, meme formats, and posting strategy for a creator brand over 6 weeks." Process credibility is still credibility.
// Your shortcut
Show, don't tell
Build 3 sample memes for the brand you're pitching BEFORE you send the message. Attach them. That's a portfolio of 3 in 2 hours.
// Pro move
Ask Aarush for a line
A one-sentence testimonial from Aarush is worth more than 10 bullet points. "The Engage Craft made content that actually got shared." Simple.
02
Instagram DM Pitches
Best for: D2C brands, music artists, creator brands
// Instagram DM Rules

Keep it under 5 lines. Instagram DMs are read on phones between scrolls. If they need to tap "see more," you've lost them. No paragraphs. No essays. One observation + one idea + one question.

Instagram
Template IG-01 · Opening DM · For D2C Brands
Tone: Direct, curious, confident
// Message (copy + fill brackets)
Hey [First Name] 👋 Noticed [Brand Name] has [X]K followers but the engagement doesn't match — and I think I know exactly why. Your content is polished but it's not making people stop scrolling. We run meme-native campaigns for brands in your space and the difference in reach is night and day. We just did this for a creator brand — happy to share what worked. Worth a quick chat?
// Example filled in (for Snitch)
Hey Siddharth 👋 Noticed Snitch has 600K followers but the engagement doesn't match — and I think I know exactly why. Your content is polished but it's not making people stop scrolling. We run meme-native campaigns for fashion brands and the difference in reach is night and day. Idea for you: "Indian Man Dress Code Decoder" — a weekly meme series on confusing dress codes solved by Snitch outfits. Easy to make, insanely shareable. Worth a quick chat?
⏱Send time: Tuesday–Thursday, 11am–1pm or 7pm–9pm. Avoid Mondays and Fridays.
📎Pro move: Attach 1 sample meme you made for them. Visual proof > 100 words.
🚫Don't: Send a voice note. Don't write more than this. Don't say "I hope this finds you well."
Instagram
Template IG-02 · Opening DM · For Music Artists / Labels
Tone: Fan-adjacent, industry-aware
// Message (copy + fill brackets)
Hey [Name] "[Song Name]" deserves way more streams than it's getting — the problem isn't the music, it's the distribution. Most artists miss the meme layer. That's where songs actually go viral on Instagram right now. We build that layer — seeding tracks across meme pages, Reels audio boosts, creator placements. We worked with Aarush Bhola's pages and saw what the right content format does to reach. If you're open to it, I'd love to show you a quick campaign idea I have for [Song Name] specifically. No pitch deck, just an idea.
🎯Key move: Name the specific song. Artists will always read a message about their own music.
💬Ending: "No pitch deck, just an idea" — removes pressure and sounds human, not salesy.
Instagram
Template IG-03 · Follow-up DM · If no reply in 4–5 days
Tone: Relaxed, no pressure
// Follow-up message
Hey [Name] — just bumping this up in case it got buried 👆 Made a quick sample meme for [Brand] to show what I mean. No strings attached, keep it either way. [Attach the sample meme here] Happy to send the full idea if it's useful.
🧠Why this works: You gave them something valuable for free. Now they feel like they owe a reply — even just a "this is cool."
03
LinkedIn DM Pitches
Best for: Founders, marketing heads, D2C brand decision-makers
// LinkedIn DM Rules

LinkedIn DMs can be slightly longer — 7–10 lines max. People on LinkedIn are in "professional mode" so you can lead with a sharper business insight. Connect first with a note, then DM. Always reference something specific from their profile or company page.

LinkedIn
Template LI-01 · Connection Request Note
Character limit: 300 · Keep it tight
// Connection note (sent with request)
Hi [Name] — been following [Brand]'s growth closely. I work in meme marketing and viral content for consumer brands — noticed a specific gap in your social strategy I think is costing you real reach. Would love to connect and share a thought.
✅Rule: Never pitch in the connection note. Just create intrigue. The pitch comes after they accept.
LinkedIn
Template LI-02 · First DM after connection · For Founders
Tone: Strategic, peer-to-peer, no fluff
// Message (copy + fill brackets)
Hey [Name] — thanks for connecting. Quick observation: [Brand] is spending on ads and influencers but there's a layer missing — meme-native content that makes people share, not just see. Your [X]K followers aren't engaging because the content is beautiful but not culturally sticky. That's a distribution problem, not a product problem. At The Engage Craft we solve exactly this — we run meme campaigns, viral seeding, and social engagement for D2C brands. We recently worked on content for creator Aarush Bhola's fanbase and the difference in organic reach was significant. I have a specific campaign idea for [Brand] — took me 20 mins to build it, takes you 2 mins to read it. Want me to send it over?
// Example filled in (for Pilgrim)
Hey Anurag — thanks for connecting. Quick observation: Pilgrim is spending on ads and influencers but there's a layer missing — meme-native content that makes people share, not just see. Your 430K followers aren't engaging because the content is polished but not culturally sticky. That's a distribution problem, not a product problem. At The Engage Craft we solve exactly this. I have a specific campaign idea called "Gori twacha ka French connection" — a meme series where Indian skin interviews its international ingredients. Took me 20 mins to build the concept, takes you 2 mins to read it. Want me to send it over?
💼Why "2 mins to read it" works: You're removing the fear of commitment. It's not a meeting or a call — it's just reading. Low friction = high reply rate.
📊If they're a marketing head (not founder): Lead with "This could be a quick win you can take to your leadership with actual projected numbers."
LinkedIn
Template LI-03 · DM for Marketing Managers (not founders)
Tone: Collaborative, makes them look good internally
// Message
Hi [Name], I do meme marketing and viral content for consumer brands — ran a quick audit on [Brand]'s socials and spotted a gap that I think is a quick win for your team. [Brand]'s audience is exactly right for meme-native content — but right now the feed is polished without being shareable. I've seen this pattern before and it's usually a 4-week fix. Takes 2 mins to read, and if it's useful, great — if not, no hard feelings at all. Would that be helpful?
🎖Why this works for managers: You're making it easy for them to say yes — it's just reading a document. And if they like it, they can bring it to their boss as their own discovery. You're giving them a win.
04
Cold Email Pitches
Best for: Brands with public contact info, music labels, event companies
// Cold Email Rules

Subject line is 80% of your email. If it doesn't open, nothing else matters. Never say "partnership opportunity" or "collaboration." Write a subject line that sounds like it was written by a human who noticed something specific. Body: max 150 words. One ask at the end.

Email
Template EM-01 · The "I noticed something" email · D2C Brands
Open rate target: 35%+ with this subject line style
// Subject line options (pick one, fill brackets)
Option A: Quick thought on [Brand]'s Instagram Option B: [Brand] has 600K followers — here's why engagement is low Option C: A meme idea for [Brand] (took me 20 mins, sharing for free)
// Email body
Hi [Name], I run The Engage Craft — we do meme marketing and viral content for D2C brands. I spent 10 minutes looking at [Brand]'s social pages and noticed your content is well-made but not getting shared. The reason: it's polished, not culturally sticky. I have a campaign idea called "[Your Campaign Idea Name]" — built specifically for [Brand]. It's ready to go. We worked with creator Aarush Bhola on fan-page content — happy to share what worked if useful. Can I send you the concept? It's a 1-page idea, not a 40-slide deck. — [Your Name] The Engage Craft
📧"1-page idea, not a 40-slide deck" — this line removes the #1 fear of replying to cold emails (being put through a long sales process).
🔍Finding emails: Use Hunter.io (free tier), or LinkedIn → "Contact Info." Format guesses: firstname@brand.com, firstname.lastname@brand.com, hello@brand.com
Email
Template EM-02 · For Music Labels / Artist Managers
Tone: Industry-savvy, results-focused
// Subject line
Option A: Getting [Song Name] on 50 meme pages — here's the plan Option B: [Artist Name]'s streams could be 3x higher. Here's why they aren't.
// Email body
Hi [Name], I work in viral music distribution — specifically the meme and Reels layer that most labels skip. "[Song Name]" has a hook that should be soundtracking thousands of Reels. It isn't, because nobody seeded it into the right meme ecosystem. Here's what I'd do: identify 40–60 meme and creator pages that match the song's emotion, seed the audio with content hooks, and run a 3-week Reels audio campaign. We've done content work for creator pages with strong organic reach. Happy to map out the exact strategy for [Artist Name] on a quick call. 10 minutes? Happy to work around your calendar. — [Your Name], The Engage Craft
🎵Why "40–60 meme pages" works: It sounds specific and planned, not vague "we'll promote your music." Specificity = credibility.

The Follow-Up Sequence
80% of replies come after follow-up #2
// The Rule

No reply doesn't mean no interest. It means they're busy, they forgot, or the timing was wrong. Follow up exactly 3 times. After that, move on — but stay connected (like their posts, comment genuinely). The deal often comes 3 months later.

Timing
Action
What to say / send
Day 1First touch
Send first message
Use IG-01, LI-02, or EM-01 templates. Always include the specific campaign idea. Don't send on Monday morning or Friday afternoon.
Day 5No reply
Value bump
"Hey [Name] — just bumping this. Made a quick sample [meme/concept] for [Brand] while I was thinking about it. Dropping it here — no obligation, keep it either way." → Attach the sample creative.
Day 12Still nothing
Soft close
"Hey — last message, I promise. Just wanted to know if now isn't the right time (totally fine) or if my idea missed the mark (useful to know). Either way, keep the sample — it's yours."
Day 30Optional
Reconnect hook
Comment genuinely on one of their posts. Then 2 days later: "Saw your post about [X] — it's exactly the content moment I was talking about. Still here if the timing works better now."
Day 60+Long game
Stay warm
Don't pitch. Like posts. Share their content with a genuine comment. When they launch something new, send: "Congrats on [launch] — I had an idea for exactly this moment. Can I share it quickly?"
06
When They Reply With An Objection
The 5 most common blocks — handled
"We already have a team handling social."
Say: "Totally makes sense — we're not here to replace them. We work alongside in-house teams to add the meme and viral distribution layer that most agencies don't specialise in. Happy to share what that looks like in practice?"
"Can you share your portfolio / past work?"
Say: "Of course — we worked with creator Aarush Bhola's fan pages on content strategy and meme-native formats. I also built [3 sample memes for YOUR brand] — I'll send those right now. They'll tell you more than any case study doc." → Then send the samples immediately.
"What are your prices / rates?"
Say: "Depends on scope — we do everything from single campaign concepts to full monthly retainers. Before we talk numbers, let me quickly show you the campaign idea I built for you. Once you see it, pricing will make much more sense in context." → Redirect to the idea, not the invoice.
"Not right now, maybe later."
Say: "No pressure at all. Can I ask — is it timing or is my idea not quite the right fit? That'll help me know whether to follow up in a month or go back to the drawing board." → Their answer tells you exactly what to do next.
"How are you different from other agencies?"
Say: "Most agencies post content. We build cultural moments — meme-native campaigns, viral seeding, engagement that compounds. The difference is whether your posts get seen or shared. I'd rather show you than explain it — give me 5 minutes to walk you through the idea I built for [Brand]?"
07
The Hard Rules
Things that kill pitches vs. things that close them
✓ Always Do
→Name a specific post, product, or number from their brand to show you actually looked
→Lead with their problem before you mention yourself or your agency
→Include 1 ready campaign idea — named, specific, with a format description
→Make your one ask crystal clear at the end of every message
→Follow up exactly 3 times then let it breathe
→Attach or offer sample creative — this is your fastest credibility shortcut
→Send Tuesday–Thursday, late morning or early evening
✗ Never Do
→Open with "I hope this message finds you well" or "My name is X and I am..."
→Send a wall of text — if it needs scrolling, they won't read it
→Use words like "synergy," "leverage," "growth hacking," "innovative solutions"
→Ask for a call before you've given them anything of value first
→Send the same generic pitch to every brand — they can feel it
→Follow up more than 3 times in the first month — it kills trust
→Apologize for reaching out or say "sorry to bother you"
