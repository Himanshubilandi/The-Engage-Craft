How to approach — 5 step playbook
1

Build your media kit first

Before reaching out to anyone, create a one-page PDF showing your creator network stats — total reach, niches covered, avg engagement rate, past brand deals.

💡 Mention "100+ tech & AI creators" prominently. Numbers open doors.
2

Find the right contact

For startups: email the founder or Head of Marketing directly. Use LinkedIn to find them. For Series B+ companies: look for "Influencer Marketing Manager" or "Brand Partnerships".

💡 Tools: Apollo.io, Hunter.io, LinkedIn Sales Navigator for finding emails free.
3

Send a warm cold email

Keep it under 100 words. Lead with their pain point, not your pitch. Show you understand their product. Offer a pilot campaign with 2–3 creators at a startup-friendly rate.

💡 Subject line formula: "100 tech creators + [CompanyName] = ?" gets high open rates.
4

Follow up on LinkedIn

Connect with their marketing team on LinkedIn the same day you email. Comment on their posts, engage with their content for 3–5 days before following up on email.

💡 Warmth before follow-up = 3x reply rate. Don't be a stranger when you follow up.
5

Propose a pilot, not a contract

Close with a low-risk pilot: 2–3 creators, 30-day campaign, performance-based pricing. Remove all friction. Once they see results, upsell the full network.

💡 Say "pilot" not "contract". Startups hate commitment but love testing.
